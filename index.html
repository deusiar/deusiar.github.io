Primary Cell Culture Growth

This study examines the potential of certain drugs to treat spinal cord injuries using a rat cortical neuron model. Neurons were isolated, cultured to form functional networks, intentionally injured to simulate trauma, and treated with various drugs to evaluate their ability to promote repair and regrowth. The hypothesis suggests that cortical neurons share repair mechanisms with spinal cord neurons, making this model relevant for identifying therapeutic candidates. Effective drugs from this study could guide spinal cord injury research, uncover molecular pathways of neural repair, and lead to new treatment strategies. Future steps include testing in spinal cord-specific models, studying underlying mechanisms, and progressing to preclinical and clinical trials. This research aims to bridge the gap between experimental findings and practical therapies for spinal cord injuries.

Rat Ultrasonic Vocalizations Behavior

The project studies young rats' behavioral responses to cocaine, saline (control), and amphetamine, along with their ultrasonic vocalizations (USVs), to assess signs of depression or addiction.

This study examines the effects of cocaine, amphetamine, and saline (control) on young rats, focusing on behavioral changes and ultrasonic vocalizations (USVs). Behavioral observations include locomotion, grooming, and social activity, while USVs are analyzed to assess emotional states—negative (22 kHz) or positive (50 kHz). The study aims to identify indicators of depression (e.g., reduced positive USVs, social withdrawal) and addiction (e.g., approach behavior to drug cues, elevated positive USVs). Ethical protocols, robust experimental design, and accurate data analysis are emphasized.

Synthesis and Crystal Structures of Supramolecular compleses of Bis-Thiamacrocycles.

This thesis focused on the synthesis and separation of two bis-macrocycle regioisomers and their complexes with AgPF₆, HgBr₂, and Pb(ClO₄)₂. Particular emphasis was placed on the distinct conformational behaviors of the isomers, which led to significant findings, including one immediate first-author publication.
